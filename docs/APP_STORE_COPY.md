## Description

> **Stop panic-googling in the store aisle.**
>
> Point Mamama at any product barcode and get a clear answer in seconds: fine for where you
> are, worth caution, or better avoided — with the medical reasoning behind it, not forum
> guesses.
>
> **MADE FOR YOUR EXACT STAGE**
> What's fine in trimester three may not be in trimester one, and nursing changes the rules
> again. Set your stage once (or add your due date and we'll track it for you) — every
> verdict adjusts automatically.
>
> **REASONS, NOT RUMORS**
> Every rating cites published guidance from sources like MotherToBaby, the NIH lactation
> database (LactMed), ACOG, the FDA, NHS, and EFSA. Tap any ingredient to see why it's
> rated the way it is — and the sources, so you can bring them to your provider.
>
> **HONEST WHEN DATA IS THIN**
> We never stamp "safe" on something we can't verify. If evidence is limited, Mamama says
> "limited data" — because you deserve honesty, not false comfort.
>
> **WHAT YOU CAN SCAN**
> • Food & drinks — additives, sweeteners, caffeine, herbal ingredients, and more
> • Skincare & cosmetics — retinoids, salicylic acid, chemical sunscreens, essential oils
> • New categories added continuously, guided by what mothers actually scan
>
> **PRIVATE BY DESIGN**
> Scans stay on your phone. No ads, no trackers, no selling data — your pregnancy is not a
> data product. No account required.
>
> Scanning and verdicts are free. Mamama Premium (optional) supports independent database
> updates and unlocks extras as they launch.
>
> ---
> Mamama is an informational tool, not medical advice. Always consult your doctor, midwife,
> or pharmacist about products and ingredients during pregnancy or while nursing.

## App Review notes (paste into the Review Information box)

> Mamama is an informational decision-support tool for expecting and nursing mothers. It is
> not a medical device and provides no diagnosis or treatment. Verdicts summarize published
> guidance from MotherToBaby (OTIS), the NIH Drugs and Lactation Database (LactMed), ACOG,
> FDA, NHS and EFSA; every ingredient rating cites its sources in-app, and a "not medical
> advice — consult your healthcare provider" disclaimer appears on every verdict screen.
> Product data comes from the public Open Food Facts / Open Beauty Facts databases.
> Uncertain results display as "Limited data" rather than a safety claim.
>
> DEMO WITHOUT A PHYSICAL PRODUCT: on the Scan tab, tap "Type barcode" and enter
> 3017620422003 (a food product) or 9002490100070 (an energy drink, which demonstrates a
> "Not recommended" verdict).
>
> No account is required to use the app. Accounts are optional (history backup) and can be
> deleted in-app at Profile → Delete account & data.
